My Story

To my surprise, I recently found out that simple website I created as a high school student - a long time ago - is still alive. Back then, I enjoyed spending time on my computer, watching sports, writing about sports, and I combined all those interests together into sporting news website. At that time, there weren't many similar content out there and I remember that even my incredibly simple page had regular visitors. I have been maintaining the page and updating the content for around six months before my computer died. I didn't get new one for a long time and, unfortunately, my enthusiasm for the website had waned.


I didn't care about learning in high school and had no ambition to do anything meaningful in my life. That's why I chose easy path that led me to enroll at University of Belgrade's Faculty of Geography - Department of Tourism. It wasn't too challenging, I graduated and later attended several courses in order to improve my practical skills. However, I didn't work in the tourism industry after all.


Instead, my career has been sports-related - I worked as sports journalist, statistician, scout, odds compiler... Some five years ago, I started coding as a hobby after discovering websites like Coursera, edX and Udemy. I found many interesting courses and really liked some of them, especially programming courses. Fast forward to 2020 and start of the pandemic - sports practically stopped and I decided it is time for change. My decision was to pursue a career in programming and began working on my coding skills seriously.

02
Skills

Python was the first programming language that I started to learn thanks to Dr. Chuck's Python for Everybody course. I also had hands-on experience working with C, as part of Harvard's CS50: Introduction to Computer Science, but haven't built on that. Since my primary field of interest is web development, I spend most of the time learning JavaScript. Besides Vanilla JS, I am working on improving my React skills. Obviously, I have knowledge of HTML/CSS/Sass and my preferred CSS framework is Bootstrap. I am pretty comfortable with using Python's Django and Node.js/Express on the backend. Additionally, I am familiar with SQL. MySQL and SQLite are my favourite relational database management systems.

Problem solving is important part of my skill set. I enjoy solving algorithm challenges at Codewars website and that practically became my daily routine. Currently, I am ranked in top 5 percent among all developers who are solving katas at Codewars.

I am on continuous learning journey and I am doing my best to improve as a developer with each passing day. Apart from working on above mentioned skills, my next goal is to add TypeScript to the list of languages I am comfortable working with. Of course, I am more than willing to learn any new language/framework/technology needed to get the job done.
